Speak English Confidently at Work

Your work speaks. You don't. If you're a Filipino professional who knows your job but goes quiet in English meetings, this is the gap Speaki was built to close.

You're good at what you do. You have ideas worth sharing. But in meetings — especially with foreign clients or leadership — you hold back. Someone else says your point first. You stay quiet on calls. You rehearse what to say, then the moment passes. Sound familiar?

This is one of the most under-addressed career problems for Filipino professionals. It's not about your competence and it's not about your grammar. It's about confidence in the moment of speaking — and that can be trained.

Where it shows up at work

  • Meetings: you have a point but wait too long, then it's too late.
  • Presentations: you freeze or rush, and your real expertise doesn't land.
  • Client calls: you sound less sure in English than you actually are.
  • 1:1s with leadership: you under-sell your work because speaking up feels risky.
You know what you want to say. You just don't say it. Over time, that silence costs promotions, raises, and recognition you've earned.

Why competent people stay quiet

When you feel evaluated — by a manager, a client, a roomful of colleagues — your stress response narrows your recall and makes speaking feel risky. So you default to silence, which feels safer in the moment but quietly limits your career. The fix isn't more English study. It's repeated, low-pressure speaking practice that retrains your response until speaking up feels normal.

How to speak up more confidently in meetings

  • Get in early. The longer you wait, the higher the barrier. Speaking in the first few minutes makes the rest easier.
  • Have entry phrases ready. "Can I add to that?" / "One thing I'd flag is…" give you a confident way in.
  • Aim for clear, not perfect. Senior people value clarity and substance, not flawless grammar.
  • Practise out loud regularly. Confidence in real meetings is built by speaking often in safe ones.
