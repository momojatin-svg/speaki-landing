English Speaking Confidence for Students & Fresh Graduates

From class recitation to your first job interview — if speaking English in front of others makes your heart race, you can build real confidence before it counts.

Many Filipino students are strong in written English and reading comprehension, but freeze during recitation, reporting, or oral defense. And just as you graduate, the stakes jump: suddenly you're expected to interview and present in confident English for jobs. That jump catches a lot of fresh graduates off guard.

The earlier you build speaking confidence, the bigger the head start you carry into your career. Speaki is designed to help you do exactly that.

Where students struggle with spoken English

  • Recitation: you know the answer but freeze when called on.
  • Reporting & presentations: nerves make you rush or go blank.
  • Group discussions: you have ideas but let louder classmates speak.
  • Oral defense & interviews: high pressure turns prepared answers into silence.
It's not that you don't know the words. It's that being watched makes them harder to reach. That's normal — and it's trainable.

Build confidence before you graduate

The students who thrive in job interviews aren't the ones with the best grammar — they're the ones who've spoken English out loud, often, in front of others, until it stopped feeling scary. You can build that muscle now, while the stakes are low, so it's ready when the stakes are high.

Simple habits that help

  • Speak English daily, even alone. Narrate your day, summarise a lesson out loud.
  • Volunteer to recite early. Going first, before nerves build, gets easier with reps.
  • Practise presentations out loud, fully. Reading slides silently doesn't prepare your voice.
  • Join a safe speaking group. Regular low-stakes practice with real people is the fastest path.
